## Methodology

### Data Preparation
- Loaded and profiled both datasets (shape, nulls, duplicates)
- Cleaned messy timestamps using a multi-format fallback parser (handles Unix ms/s, IST label, mixed date strings)
- Aggregated trades to daily level: total PnL, win rate, trade count, avg leverage, avg size, long/short ratio, drawdown proxy
- Left-joined trading metrics onto Fear/Greed index by date

### Metrics Engineered
| Metric | Definition |
|---|---|
| `total_pnl` | Sum of closedPnL per day |
| `win_rate` | % of trades with positive PnL |
| `drawdown_proxy` | Absolute value of negative-PnL days |
| `avg_leverage` | Mean leverage per day |
| `avg_size` | Mean trade notional per day |
| `trade_count` | Number of executions per day |
| `long_short_ratio` | Long trades ÷ short trades per day |

## Key Insights

**Insight 1 – PnL differs materially across sentiment regimes**
Greed days produce significantly higher mean daily PnL than Fear days.
The swing between best and worst regimes confirms sentiment is an
economically meaningful signal, not just noise.

**Insight 2 – Win rate tracks sentiment**
Win rates on Fear days fall below the 50% random baseline while Greed
days consistently exceed it — traders pick better entries in positive
sentiment environments.

**Insight 3 – Traders over-leverage during Fear, amplifying losses**
Average leverage is highest on Fear days despite those being the
worst-performing days. High-leverage traders show disproportionately
worse drawdowns in fear regimes compared to low-leverage peers.

## Strategy Recommendations

### Strategy 1 – Sentiment-Gated Position Sizing
| Regime | Leverage Multiplier | Trade Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Extreme Fear | 0.5× | –50% |
| Fear | 0.75× | –25% |
| Neutral | 1.0× | Normal |
| Greed | 1.25× | +10% |
| Extreme Greed | 0.8× | –20% |

Reduces exposure exactly when statistical edge is weakest.

### Strategy 2 – Contrarian Entry on Fear Streaks
**Trigger:** Fear/Greed ≤ Fear for ≥ 2 consecutive days AND prior day PnL < 10th percentile
**Action:** Open contrarian long at day-open, close at day-close (1.0× leverage only, no overnight hold)
**Rationale:** Extended fear + overselling historically precedes short-term mean-reversion bounces on Hyperliquid.
